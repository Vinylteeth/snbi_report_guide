  • Wide cracking

  • Widespread moderate to major spalling

  • Deep spalling to the second layer of rebar

  • Heavy corrosion with notable section loss in the reinforcing steel

  • Moderate to major scour and/or erosion

  • Flow restricted by 50-60%

  • Sinkholes in the shoulder indicating loss of fill

  • Structurally significant deformations

3 (12-month frequency)

  • Strength and/or performance is seriously affected

  • Moderate to major loss of bearing

  • Crushing of concrete

  • Significant spalling and deterioration

  • Some major scour and/or erosion

  • Flow restricted by 60%-70%

2 (12-month frequency)

  • Collapse does not present a serious threat to the public

  • Actively collapsing or filling in

  • Buried

1 (12-month frequency)

  • Requires immediate action to keep the road open

  • Presents a serious threat to the public

0 (Inspected as needed)

  • Requires immediate action to close the road

  • Cannot be repaired and must be replaced

Notes:

  • Slightly less consideration should be given for the end portions not under fill.

  • The difference between a 2 and a 1 is largely based on the amount of cover. A structure that has enough cover that a collapse would not impact the roadway can be rated a 2. A structure whose collapse would impact the roadway must be rated a 1, and action must be taken immediately upon discovery.

Preferred CV Suffixes and Naming Conventions:

  • SB, NB, EB, WB: Under southbound, northbound, eastbound, or westbound only. This is for culverts that have an opening in the median.

  • SBR, NBR, EBR, WBR: Under southbound, northbound, eastbound, or westbound ramp only.

  • ADJ: Adjacent to the road (can add NB, SB, EB, WB to designate directional travel side of the road, e.g., NBADJ would mean the culvert is under a road adjacent to the northbound lanes)

  • CR: One end is under a County Road (can add NB, SB, EB, WB to designate directional travel side of the road, e.g., NBCR would mean the culvert runs under a county road adjacent to the northbound lanes)

  • SPC: Special notes on the location can be found in the report (catch basin, only has one open end, part of a complex system, confined space, etc.)

  • Combinations are possible: SB-SBCR, NB-NBR, EBR-EBCR, WBADJ-SPC

Writing the report:

  • Change the to and from report dates in the report

  • Update the Culvert Rating and Channel Rating along with their comments

    • Add maintenance items to your comments (i.e. A deficiency was submitted for erosion)

  • Add any special notes to the Inspection Summary

    • Prefer items that do not change and that you would share with anyone (i.e. location)

  • Add any team communication notes to the comments box under the Task Info tab

    • o Parking, best way down, hazards, temporary conditions or things you might not want to advertise (i.e. high water, programmed, under construction, cannot locate north end)

    • Not needed on most structures

  • If it is a 4 or below you can check SPMS to see if it is programmed

    • If not, add it to a list to be sent to Asset

    • If yes, add the word programmed to the comments box under the Task Info tab

      • The designation number and date are not valuable because they could change

After you have processed the report go to the asset view:

  • Add a photo to the asset and set it as the default

    • Can drag a photo from the browser tab to the add many screen

    • View the photo and click the 3 dots to set as default

  • Update the inspection date and inspection frequency

    • Only full concrete rated 7 or higher can be 72 months

  • Update the material type (Kind of Material)

    • Steel

    • Concrete

    • Masonry

    • Aluminum, Wrought Iron, Cast Iron

    • Polymer Lined

    • * Use steel if it is both steel and concrete so frequencies can be verified

Helpful updates for identification:

  • Update the GPS if you know it is wrong

    • On interstates where the CV goes under both directions, put it on the up-station side (NB side or the EB side).

    • On SRs the middle of the road is fine unless there is a median or split, then put it on the up-station side.

  • Update the structure type (Type of Structure)

    • Use other for CVs with additional treatments

  • Update the skew if it is significantly skewed and the skew makes it easier to find or identify

    • 0 is perpendicular to the road

    • 45 is significant and would help identify the structure in the field

    • 90 is parallel to the road (adjacent)
